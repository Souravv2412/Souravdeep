Situation
A growing retail operations company was processing 80 to 120 vendor invoices per month with no standard template. Every invoice looked different depending on who created it, which led to frequent calculation errors, missing payment terms, and an accounts payable backlog running 30 days late. Financial reporting took a coordinator three full days every month.
Task and Objective
Design a unified financial data architecture in Excel that standardized invoice creation, automated all calculations, organized transaction history into a queryable data model, and produced a live executive dashboard that replaced the manual monthly report.
Action
What I Built and How
1
Architected a Star Schema data model in Excel with a Master Transaction Log as the central Fact Table, capturing every invoice by Invoice ID, Vendor ID, Department Code, amount, tax, due date, and payment status.
2
Built four Dimension Tables: Vendor Directory, Department Cost Centers, Tax Rate Reference, and Payment Status Reference, each linked to the central log.
3
Created an invoice template with locked formula cells for subtotal, tax, and total calculations so no one could accidentally overwrite a calculation.
4
Applied conditional formatting to flag overdue invoices in red automatically and added a live Days Outstanding formula to every transaction row.
5
Built an executive dashboard with four pivot table views and three KPI cards showing total payables, overdue amount, and payment status breakdown.
Process Diagram

Result
The Outcome
Monthly reporting time dropped from three days to 3.5 hours. Invoice calculation errors fell to zero in the first month due to locked formulas and dropdown validation. The accounts payable backlog cleared within six weeks. The vendor tier analysis identified two suppliers responsible for 60% of all disputed invoices.
