Situation
A C-suite executive was managing relationships across four time zones with a support team of three and no unified scheduling protocol. Double-bookings happened twice a month. High-priority client calls were being lost to internal meetings that could easily have been rescheduled. The executive estimated spending 8 hours every week resolving calendar conflicts reactively.
Task and Objective
Design a proactive scheduling architecture using Microsoft Outlook and Calendly that would protect the executive's focus time, eliminate conflict resolution entirely, and give all stakeholders real-time visibility into availability.
Action
What I Built and How
1
Built a three-tier meeting classification system (Client-Facing, Internal Review, Strategic Planning) with dedicated time caps, color codes, and buffer rules for each type.
2
Configured mandatory 15-minute prep blocks before all external meetings and 10-minute post-note windows after, built directly into the calendar structure.
3
Set up tiered Calendly booking links: one for Tier 1 clients, one for Tier 2/3 clients, and one for internal staff with separate duration limits.
4
Automated reminders at 24 hours and 30 minutes before each meeting using custom confirmation templates per meeting type.
5
Built a Star Schema tracking system in Excel: Executive Master Calendar Log (Fact Table) with Meeting Type Templates, Stakeholder Priority Matrix, and Time Zone Reference as Dimension Tables.
Process Diagram
Full scheduling workflow from meeting request to confirmed calendar entry
Result
The Outcome
Double-bookings were eliminated within the first two weeks. The executive reclaimed 8 hours per week previously lost to reactive scheduling. Client meeting prep compliance improved from 40% to 92% because prep blocks were now structural, not optional. The Calendly self-service setup cut scheduling email exchanges by an average of 6 per booking.
