A record of what I actually did as SRC Vice President - events, conferences, volunteering, and student advocacy.

College Student Alliance (CSA) Annual Conference

Toronto, Ontario  ·  May 2025  ·  3-Day Provincial Conference

Visit CSA

The College Student Alliance (CSA) is a non-profit, non-partisan organization representing the interests of approximately 40,000 full-time and part-time college students at four student associations across Ontario. I attended the CSA Annual Conference in Toronto as an SRC delegate from St. Clair College, joining student leaders from colleges across the province.

Over three days, delegates engaged in workshops, government briefings, and advocacy sessions addressing the top student issues in Ontario for 2025-2026: international student regulations, tuition and OSAP affordability, work-integrated learning, student housing, and mental health supports. I contributed to collaborative policy discussions and represented the perspective of St. Clair Downtown students.

Ontario Student Voices (OSV) Conference

Ontario  ·  2025-2026  ·  Provincial Advocacy Organization

Visit OSV

Ontario Student Voices (OSV) is a provincial advocacy organization dedicated to representing college and polytechnic students across Ontario. OSV elevates student voices to the provincial level through evidence-based policy research, advocacy to MPPs, and educational conferences. Their mission is to ensure student perspectives are included in the conversations and decisions that impact them.

I attended an OSV conference as an SRC delegate, engaging in sessions focused on provincial policy priorities and student advocacy strategies. OSV has met with over 45 MPPs, released multiple policy papers, and hosted annual advocacy weeks at Queen's Park. Attending gave me direct exposure to how student issues are escalated to government and shaped my approach to stakeholder communication and formal representation.

De-Stress Exam Day Event

St. Clair College Downtown  ·  2025-2026  ·  Student Wellness Event (Planned and Executed by me)

I planned, budgeted, promoted, and executed this student wellness event during the exam season at St. Clair Downtown Campus. The goal was simple: give students a break from exam stress with organized activities, refreshments, and a supportive environment on campus. I managed everything from the budget allocation and vendor coordination to event promotion, student registration, and post-event follow-up.

The event was promoted through campus channels and social media. Attendance was tracked through a registration form, and post-event feedback was collected through a structured follow-up form to measure satisfaction and inform future events.

United Way Summer Eats for Kids Program

Windsor, Ontario  ·  August 2025  ·  Community Volunteering with SRC and St. Clair College

Read Article

I joined representatives from St. Clair College's SRC, the Saints Student Athletic Association (SSAA), and College President Michael Silvaggi to volunteer with United Way's Summer Eats for Kids program on August 5, 2025. Our team distributed approximately 110 bags of fresh fruit, vegetables, and non-perishable food items to families in need at General Brock Public School in Windsor.

The Summer Eats for Kids program runs pop-up food banks twice a week at six Windsor locations throughout the summer, helping break down transportation barriers by bringing food resources directly into communities. St. Clair College has partnered with United Way for this initiative for five years. This experience reflected the SRC's commitment to community impact beyond the classroom.

"Our friends at St. Clair College, the SRC and SSAA are a huge part of making this work possible. Their dedication and commitment to the community through United Way has made a measurable impact on the lives of children and families in our community."
Rachel Meeke - Corporate Relationship Manager, United Way Windsor-Essex Chatham-Kent
